Original Text

Mitochondria are double-membraned organelles found in the cytoplasm of eukaryotic cells. They are often referred to as the 'powerhouses of the cell' because they generate most of the cell's supply of adenosine triphosphate (ATP), used as a source of chemical energy. Mitochondria are involved in other tasks, such as signaling, cellular differentiation, and cell death, as well as maintaining control of the cell cycle and cell growth.

Simplified Content

Mitochondria are parts of your cells that create energy. They're called the "powerhouses" because they make most of the cell's energy (called ATP). They also help with other important jobs like signaling when cells should grow or die.

Key Points:

  • Mitochondria are found in almost all cells
  • They create energy (ATP) for the cell
  • They have two membranes (outer and inner)
  • They help control cell growth and death
